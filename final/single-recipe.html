Mangalore Buns

Mangalore buns are sweet fried bread made with mashed bananas, flour, sugar and spiced with a touch of ground cumin. These sweet banana buns or poori as they are known is a specialty dish from the Mangalore region in Karnataka. They are a few ways to make them and they are traditionally made with all-purpose flour. I share a recipe made with whole wheat flour.

prep time

5 hr.

cook time

5 min.

serving

10 servings

Tags : Chicken breakfast pancakes food

instructions

step 1

Take very well ripened banana. You can use 1 big or 2 small bananas.Peel and mash the banana using a fork or using hands. Make sure that there are no bits and pieces. You can grind it in the mixie as well, along with salt and sugar.

step 2

Now to the same bowl add sugar, curd, baking soda, salt, cumin seeds and a tsp of ghee. Mix well, Now add maida flour little by little and prepare mangalore buns or banana poori dough. Let the consistency be softer then chapathi dough. At the end add one more tsp ghee and knead it again.

step 3

Now add maida flour little by little and prepare mangalore buns or banana poori dough. Let the consistency be softer then chapathi dough. At the end add one more tsp ghee and knead it again.After the fermentation if you feel the dough is very soft add 1 or 2 tsp of maida flour and kneed it again. Take a lemon sized ball, roll it to make a poori, which is little thick.

step 4

Roll out all the mangalore buns and keep it ready. Heat oil in a frying pan and deep fry the mangalore buns one by one till it gets a nice golden brown colour.Fry all the buns and serve it hot with chutney and sambar. Or serve as it is

ingredients

2 Bananas

2 tbsp Raw sugar

2 tbsp Yogurt

1/4 tsp Baking soda

1/2 tsp Salt

1 tsp Cumin seeds/Jeera

2 cups All-purpose flour

tools

Rolling pin

Frying pan
